Your Skin Guide

Understanding your skin type is the first step toward choosing the right skincare products and building a routine that truly works for you. Follow these simple guidelines to help identify your skin type:

IDENTIFY YOUR SKIN TYPE

Try different at-home tests to figure out your skin type. Below are two common methods that can help you identify which category your skin belongs to.

STEPS & METHODS

This section outlines two simple ways to identify your skin type: the “Watch and Wait” Method and the Blotting Sheet Method. Each method follows a series of steps that help you observe how your skin behaves under certain conditions.

Step Watch and Wait Method Blotting Sheet Method
1 Wash face with gentle cleanser Wash face with gentle cleanser
2 Leave skin bare for 30 mins Wait 30 mins without products
3 Pat dry with soft towel Pat dry with soft towel
4 Check skin in mirror Press blotting sheet on face
5 Observe skin condition (shine, dryness, balance) Check oil spots on blotting sheet

SKIN RESULT & INTERPRETATION

After completing the steps, the next part is to interpret the results. By comparing what you see in the mirror or on the blotting sheet, you can determine whether your skin is oily, dry, combination, or normal. This table summarizes the possible outcomes for each method.

Skin Type Watch and Wait Method Blotting Sheet Method
Oily Skin Shiny all over Lots of oil all over
Dry Skin Tight, rough, or flaky Little to no oil
Combination Skin Shine only on the T-zone Oil only on the T-zone
Normal Skin Feels balanced (not oily or dry) Small amount of oil across the face
